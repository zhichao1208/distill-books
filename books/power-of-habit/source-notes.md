# Power of Habit · sources

## Book
- *The Power of Habit*, Charles Duhigg, Random House, 2012
- ISBN: 978-1400069286 · Chinese: 《习惯的力量》, 中信出版社, 2013
- Goodreads ~4.13 / ~575k ratings · NYT bestseller (60+ weeks) · Pulitzer-finalist author (Duhigg's later journalism)
- Sales ~5M+ copies

## Chapter mapping
- Ch 1-3: Habit Loop + cravings → SKILL's core method
- Ch 5: Willpower as muscle → **NOT used** (ego depletion replication failure)
- Ch 6-7: Organizational habits (Alcoa, Starbucks) → cold-start B
- Ch 8: Movements / society → not used (out of skill scope)
- Ch 9-10: Free will + responsibility → not used

## Critical honesty
- **Ego depletion** (Ch 5 willpower-as-muscle): Baumeister's foundational research failed Hagger 2016 pre-registered 23-lab replication. Skill explicitly refuses "willpower depleted" framing.
- **Case studies are journalism**: Alcoa, Target, Pepsodent, Febreze, Phelps are story-telling, not causal evidence. Useful as illustration of the Habit Loop concept; not proof of mechanism.
- **Keystone Habits** mostly heuristic; one notable RCT (Norway 2021, exercise as keystone) supports limited claim. Most "keystone" claims are weaker than book implies.
- **The Golden Rule of Habit Change** (keep cue + reward, swap routine) has SOME experimental support but is more clinical-experience than RCT-validated.

## Concepts deliberately not used
- Willpower-as-muscle (failed replication)
- Specific organizational case studies as recommendations (Duhigg's Alcoa story can't be applied "for free" to other companies)
- Free-will / criminal-responsibility framing (Ch 10 — interesting but out of skill scope)

## Differentiation from AH / TH
PoH is the only book of the three that gives a SUBSTITUTION tool for habits you've quit and restarted. AH builds new identity habits; TH builds new behavior via celebration; PoH SWAPS routine while preserving cue + reward.
