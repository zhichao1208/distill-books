## Primary research cited in the skill

- Roediger & Karpicke (2006). Test-Enhanced Learning. *Psychological Science* 17: 249-255.
- Roediger & Karpicke (2006). The Power of Testing Memory. *Perspectives on Psychological Science* 1(3): 181-210.
- Adesope, Trevisan & Sundararajan (2017). Rethinking the Use of Tests: A Meta-Analysis of Practice Testing. *Review of Educational Research* 87(3). g ≈ 0.61-0.70.
- Rowland (2014). Meta-analysis of practice testing. g ≈ 0.50.
- Pashler, McDaniel, Rohrer & Bjork (2008). Learning Styles: Concepts and Evidence. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest* 9(3).
- Dunlosky et al. (2013). Improving Students' Learning with Effective Learning Techniques. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest* 14(1).
- Cepeda, Pashler, Vul, Wixted & Rohrer (2006). Spacing effect meta-analysis. *Psychological Bulletin.*
- Bjork & Bjork on desirable difficulties (UCLA Bjork lab).

## Critique sources used (honest-scope)

- Brunmair & Richter (2019). Similarity matters: A meta-analysis of interleaved learning. *Psychological Bulletin.* 59 studies. Material-dependence: g=0.67 (paintings), g=0.34 (math), g=0.21 ns (expository), g=-0.39 (word lists).
- Karpicke & Aue (2015). The Testing Effect Is Alive and Well with Complex Materials. *Educational Psychology Review* 27. (Rebuts Van Gog & Sweller 2015.)
- Kalyuga, Ayres, Chandler & Sweller — expertise-reversal effect.
- Storm lab — retrieval-induced forgetting.
- Daniel Willingham's FAQ on learning styles (the careful version of the rebuttal).

## Concepts NOT used and why

- **Chapter-opening narratives** (Marine officer, neurosurgeon, highway patrol trainee) — narrative-grade, not actionable; Brown's voice; not load-bearing.
- **Memory-palace / peg system mnemonics** (Ch. 8) — useful for some content (medical terminology, vocabulary lists) but not central to the book's evidence story; user can go to Joshua Foer's *Moonwalking with Einstein* for the experiential treatment.
- **"Increase your abilities" Chapter 7** — the weakest chapter scientifically (biographical case studies, lower-tier evidence); the skill uses Growth-Mindset routing via Mindset skill instead.
- **Bonnie Blair / James Patterson / Aoki golf biographical material** — narrative color, not method.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Make It Stick is the **highest-evidence-density book in the distill-books slate**. Co-authored by two working cognitive psychologists (Roediger NAS member; McDaniel CIRCLE director), not journalists alone. The skill is the cognitive/focus #3 archetype member, paired with Deep Work (focus-block infrastructure) and Stolen Focus (diagnostic of attention loss). Where Deep Work tells you HOW to focus, Make It Stick tells you what to do WITHIN the focus block to actually retain.
