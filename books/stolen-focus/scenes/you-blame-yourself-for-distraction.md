# Scene: you blame yourself for distraction

## Trigger

User uses willpower / discipline / personal failing language: "I have no willpower" / "I'm broken" / "I just need more discipline" / "I should be able to focus."

This is the most important reframe scene in the skill. Most users arrive in self-blame mode.

## The core move: re-attribute the cause

```
Quick reframe. What you've been telling yourself:

  "I have no willpower."

What's actually happening (synthesis from researchers Hari cites —
Gloria Mark, Tristan Harris, others):

  You are one human nervous system, running 8-16 hours/day against
  an environment partly engineered to defeat attention by teams of
  engineers, with revenue tied to your engagement.

Specifically:
  - Notifications fire ~63 times/day average (Gloria Mark research)
  - Each interruption costs ~23 minutes of full focus recovery
  - "Engagement-optimizing" algorithms test variants on you
    constantly, evolving toward what holds your attention against
    your will
  - Open offices, always-on culture, slack-as-default-pace
  - On top of: sleep debt, chronic stress, glucose-volatile diet,
    air pollution, screen-time, decline of long-form reading muscle

Your willpower isn't broken. It's appropriately sized for an
environment that doesn't exist anymore.

The reframe matters because it changes what you fix:
  - "I have no willpower" → fix = try harder (doesn't work)
  - "My environment defeats willpower" → fix = change environment +
    seek specific tools + accept some of this is collective, not
    just personal
```

## Where to NOT take this reframe

The reframe can be misused. Skill flags:

```
Two important caveats:

  1. The reframe is NOT "nothing is your responsibility." You still
     have agency. You can still change individual environment (delete
     apps, move phone, work different hours, choose different jobs).
     The reframe shifts WHERE you spend the agency — into design,
     not into guilt.
     
  2. The reframe is NOT a replacement for clinical assessment if you
     have functional impairment. If attention problems are wrecking
     work / relationships / time management consistently, get
     evaluated for ADHD / anxiety / depression / sleep disorder.
     "It's the environment" can become an excuse to skip clinical
     help that actually treats clinical problems.

The reframe is a tool for clearer thinking about cause, not a
license to do nothing or skip diagnosis.
```

## What changes after the reframe

```
Practical changes once you internalize the reframe:

  - Stop spending willpower on "trying harder" — that money is in
    the wrong bank
  - Spend energy on environment design (which apps, where phone is,
    work hours, calendar defenses)
  - Seek collective fixes too (vote for privacy regulation, choose
    workplaces with better defaults, support institutions doing
    real attention work)
  - Reduce guilt overhead — guilt itself eats focus budget. Less
    self-blame = more cognitive resource available

The reframe is one of those moves that does most of its work in the
first 5 minutes (the relief) and the rest over months (the redesign).
```

## Closer

```
Action this week: pick ONE specific way you've been blaming yourself
that's actually environmental, and name it. Out loud or in a note.

Examples:
  - "I'm not undisciplined for not 'just turning off Slack' — Slack
    is designed to make turning it off feel rude."
  - "I'm not weak for losing 2 hours to TikTok — the app is built
    to do this; I'm a normal human."
  - "I'm not broken for needing 8 hours of sleep — sleep debt was
    being marketed to me as ambition for a decade."

One specific reframe. Out loud or written.

Then: go to Deep Work skill for the individual operating manual,
or Tiny Habits skill for environment design. This skill (Stolen
Focus) finished its job once the reframe lands.

Come back if the reframe loosens / doesn't stick / you need to talk
about which force was biggest. Letter is enough next time.
```

---

## Failure modes

- **User uses reframe as permission to disengage entirely.** Skill should clarify: the reframe is to redirect agency, not eliminate it. "You're not off the hook — you're on the right hook now."

- **User has clinical attention issue and the reframe lets them avoid evaluation.** Skill flags: "If you have functional impairment for 3+ months, get evaluated. The reframe doesn't replace assessment for clinical conditions."

- **User says "but society won't fix this in my lifetime."** Honest acknowledgment: "Probably true for some parts. The individual + collective approach is parallel, not sequential. Do individual now; participate in collective when you can."
