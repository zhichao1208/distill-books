## Concepts NOT carried forward (and why)

- **"Body keeps the score" as literal cellular storage** — misread; biologically inaccurate; flagged in skill.
- **Specific modality recommendations as treatment plans** — clinician's call, never AI skill's.
- **De-emphasis of first-line PE/CPT/TF-CBT** — the book's stance on these is contested; the skill names them alongside body-based.
- **Case studies as evidence** — vivid but anecdotal; not the basis for individual treatment decisions.
- **MDMA-assisted therapy as currently available** — FDA rejected Aug 2024; not standard care.
- **Holotropic breathwork / drumming / theater** as recommended — weak evidence; the skill mentions only in modality map for completeness.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Body Keeps the Score is the first book in the **trauma / healing archetype** (new in batch 3) and the STRICTEST honest-scope skill in the entire slate. It exists because the book has shaped popular trauma discourse for a decade, the modality evidence varies enormously, and the misreading patterns (self-diagnosis, cellular-storage metaphor, modality enthusiasm without clinical guidance) are real risks. The skill is designed as literacy + routing — never as treatment — with crisis resources prominent at the top.

Adjacent and arguably more rigorous for some readers:
- Judith Herman, *Trauma and Recovery* (1992) — foundational, more rigorous, the C-PTSD originator. Often should be read first.
- Edna Foa, *Prolonged Exposure Therapy for PTSD* (2007) — first-line clinician manual.
- David Treleaven, *Trauma-Sensitive Mindfulness* (2018) — for mindfulness practice adapted for trauma.
- Stephanie Foo, *What My Bones Know* (2022) — humane memoir companion.
