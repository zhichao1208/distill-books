# Tiny Habits · sources and chapter mapping

## Book

- **Original**: *Tiny Habits: The Small Changes That Change Everything*
- **Author**: BJ Fogg, PhD
- **Publisher**: Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2019 (US); Virgin Books / Penguin (UK)
- **ISBN**: 978-0358003328 (hardcover) / 978-0358362777 (paperback)
- **Chinese edition**: 《福格行为模型》, 天津科学技术出版社, 2021 (titled after Fogg's Behavior Model rather than Tiny Habits — Chinese edition foregrounds the academic frame more than the consumer one)
- **Author site**: <https://www.tinyhabits.com/> and <https://www.bjfogg.com/>
- **Goodreads**: 4.13 / 5 across ~19,172 ratings as of 2026
- **Awards**: NYT bestseller; Amazon editors' #1 leadership/business book of 2020

| "Habit Recipe Cards" as physical artifact | Suggested in book; skill doesn't recommend specific paper products (commercial bias risk) |
| Tiny Habits Academy (Fogg's paid course) | Commercial; skill doesn't recommend the paid program |

| Behavior Design as corporate consulting framework | Out of skill scope (organizational); could be future skill |
| "Skill of Change" framing as identity concept | This is Fogg's territory parallel to Atomic Habits' identity-vote — the skill explicitly contrasts them rather than blending |

## Important difference vs Atomic Habits (declared openly)

The two books are often compared and conflated. The skill makes the difference explicit because users who tried one and failed often need to try the other.

| | Atomic Habits (Clear, 2018) | Tiny Habits (Fogg, 2019) |
|---|---|---|
| Engine of habit formation | Identity vote → repetition compounds | Celebration → dopamine wires the behavior |
| What's "small" for | Easier to start; identity claim | Wiring needs minimum ability + emotional reward |
| Troubleshooting starting point | 4 gears (cue/craving/response/reward) | Prompt → Ability → Motivation (in that order) |
| What to do about motivation | "Don't rely on it; build systems" | "Don't rely on it; it's the enemy of habit formation" |
| Anchor / context role | Cue design + environment design | Anchor matching (frequency/location/theme) |
| Stance on calendar tracking | Compatible with streak tracking | Rejects "X days" timelines |
| Stance on celebration | "Make it satisfying" (one of 4 laws) | Celebration is THE law; everything else is staging |

Both books are useful. Each fits a different user. The skill recommends the other when the first one doesn't land.

## Chinese edition notes

Chinese translation chooses to foreground "福格行为模型" (Fogg Behavior Model) as the title, which is technically accurate but downplays "tiny" — the central emotional hook of the English version. Skill in Chinese contexts may need to surface both framings.

Key terminology:
| English | Chinese |
|---|---|
| Tiny Habits | 微习惯 / 小习惯 (multiple usages in market) |
| B = MAP (Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt) | B = MAP (行为 = 动机 × 能力 × 提示) — formula kept in original |
| Anchor | 锚点 |
| Celebration / Shine | 庆祝 / 闪光 |
| Pearl Habit | 珍珠习惯 (literal) — works in Chinese |
| Focus Mapping | 聚焦映射 / 影响-可行性矩阵 |
| Golden Behaviors | 黄金行为 |
| ABC Recipe | ABC 配方 (literal) |

## Related / complementary books

- **Atomic Habits** (James Clear, 2018) — Counterpart; explicit contrast in main SKILL.md and in this source-notes
- **The Power of Habit** (Charles Duhigg, 2012) — Cue/Routine/Reward predecessor; less actionable than either Clear or Fogg
- **Hooked** (Nir Eyal, 2014) — Product-side habit loop; useful for understanding why bad habits stick
- **Behavior Design / Persuasive Technology** (Fogg's earlier academic work) — Background; not consumer-facing
- **Tiny Habits for Teams** (potential future Fogg book; not currently published as of 2026)

## Important limitations / counterpoints

- **The celebration requirement is non-negotiable in Fogg's frame.** Users who refuse on principle ("it's silly") are doing a different method. The skill says this openly rather than letting users believe they can skip the step and still get Fogg's results.

- **The method assumes baseline ability for felt positive emotion.** Depression, anhedonia, severe grief, and active OCD all interfere — documented in the not_for list and routed to vulnerable exits.

- **The "tiny" rule conflicts with achievement-oriented users.** Some users hear "1 push-up" and think "that's stupid, not enough." The skill defends the rule but acknowledges the user can scale up — must just respect the tiny starting point.

- **Fogg's commercial ecosystem (Tiny Habits Academy, coach certification)** raises a fair-use awareness flag. Skill cites the book + free public talks/articles, does not recommend the paid programs.

- **Pearl Habits can become procrastination prompts** if the irritant happens too often (every email = excuse to "take a break"). Scene flags this failure mode explicitly.
