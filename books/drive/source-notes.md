- Deci (1971). Effects of externally mediated rewards on intrinsic motivation.
- Lepper, Greene & Nisbett (1973). Undermining children's intrinsic interest with extrinsic reward (overjustification).

## Concepts NOT used and why

- **"Motivation 3.0" branding** — useful frame but feels dated 16 years on; the skill uses SDT directly.
- **Best Buy ROWE as live case** — dismantled 2013; carry only with explicit walkback.
- **Google "20% time" as live case** — substantially curtailed per Quartz 2013; carry only with caveat.
- **Pink's binary intrinsic-vs-extrinsic collapse** — the skill uses Cerasoli 2014's "largely independent" framing instead.
- **The seven-flaws enumeration** — most are sourced from Deci/Ryan/Lepper, which are stronger; skill uses the original research.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Drive is the career/motivation archetype member #3, complementing Range (match-quality) and So Good (career capital). Where Range answers "what should I do" and So Good answers "how do I build the skill," Drive answers "given I'm doing this work, what makes the doing of it engaging?" The book is also the most accessible translation of Self-Determination Theory available to general managers. The skill exists because the popular framing is too binary (incentives = always bad), and the operational caveats (work type, floor conditions, walked-back flagship cases) need explicit handling.
