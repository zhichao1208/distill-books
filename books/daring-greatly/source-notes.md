# Daring Greatly · sources

## Book
- *Daring Greatly*, Brené Brown, Avery, 2012
- ISBN: 978-1592408412 · Chinese: 《脆弱的力量》, 北方文艺出版社, 2014
- Brown: U Houston social work professor / researcher
- Goodreads ~4.27 / ~115k ratings · ~4M+ copies
- TED talk "The Power of Vulnerability" (2010, 60M+ views) preceded book
- Netflix special (2019), Atlas of the Heart book (2021), Dare to Lead (2018) follow-ups

## Core methodology
- Vulnerability redefined: uncertainty + risk + emotional exposure (NOT weakness)
- "Vulnerability is the birthplace of innovation, creativity, change"
- Shame vs Guilt distinction (shame = "I AM bad"; guilt = "I DID something bad")
- Shame resilience (4 elements: recognize triggers, critical awareness, reach out, speak shame)
- Wholehearted living (10 guideposts)
- Scarcity culture ("never enough")
- Arena metaphor (Roosevelt's "Man in the Arena" speech)
- Vulnerability armor: foreboding joy / perfectionism / numbing
- Disengagement in relationships (key relationship-killer)
- Leadership: daring leaders create cultures of belonging

## Critical honesty (LOAD-BEARING)
- **"Vulnerability Inc." / vulnerability theater** — concept has become corporate buzzword. Curated story-sharing as performance, not actual risk, is now common in workplace settings. By Brown's own definition this isn't vulnerability.
- **Research methodology** (grounded theory from ~1200 interviews, ~11,000 coded phrases) is solid for theory-building from a specific sample. Popular-book voice universalizes claims her academic papers qualify.
- **Privilege / scope** — primarily US, white, middle-class sample. Cross-cultural applicability less examined.
- **ORDER MATTERS** — vulnerability without trust is danger, not strength. Brown herself says this; popular reception sometimes forgets.
- **Disability / trauma intersection** — chronic shame from childhood abuse / complex trauma needs specialist (van der Kolk territory), not Brown's framework.

## Concepts deliberately not used
- "Vulnerability fixes everything" framing (it doesn't)
- Workplace-vulnerability-on-demand (often unsafe)
- "Wholehearted" as prescriptive ("if you're not wholehearted you're doing it wrong")
- Atlas of the Heart's expanded emotion taxonomy (separate book; out of skill scope for v0.5)
- Dare to Lead workplace content (planned for future skill)
