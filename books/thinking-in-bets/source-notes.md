# Thinking in Bets · sources

## Book
- *Thinking in Bets*, Annie Duke, Portfolio / Penguin, 2018
- ISBN: 978-0735216358 · Chinese: 《对赌》, 北京联合出版公司, 2019
- Author: Annie Duke — World Series of Poker bracelet, $4M+ career winnings, PhD work in cognitive psychology paused for poker
- Goodreads ~3.94 / ~25k ratings
- Follow-up: How to Decide (2020), Quit (2022)

## Core methodology
- **"Resulting"** — the bias of judging decision QUALITY by outcome QUALITY (the load-bearing concept)
- All decisions are bets (probabilistic, even "sure" things)
- Separating skill from luck in outcomes
- Decision tree thinking (branches with probabilities)
- Calibrated confidence (70% means 70%, not "pretty sure")
- "How sure am I" hedging language
- Bet-against-yourself technique (would you take the other side?)
- Truthseeking pods (small group committed to challenging beliefs)
- Backcasting (premortem variation)
- Mental time travel (10/10/10-inspired)

## Critical honesty
- **Poker-derived metaphor distancing in non-poker domains** — readers in messy human contexts sometimes find the analogy too clean.
- **"Resulting" reframe is genuinely useful** and broadly applicable; some of the more elaborate decision-tree work is harder to apply in messy real life.
- **Truthseeking pod assumes social context** many readers don't have.
- **Poker has clean outcome attribution** that most life decisions don't — the analogy oversells in some domains.
- **Duke has commercial enterprise** (Alliance for Decision Education, How to Decide consulting) — note for fair-use awareness.
- **Resulting cuts both ways** — sometimes bad outcome IS evidence the decision was flawed. The skill flags this risk (rationalization trap).

## Differentiation from Decisive
- **Decisive = structural / one-off**: WRAP framework applied to ONE specific consequential decision
- **Thinking in Bets = probabilistic / repeated**: calibration across many bets; resulting check on past decisions

Use Decisive for job offer / move / partner choice. Use Thinking in Bets for hiring policy / investment strategy / product bets where you'll be evaluated across many outcomes.

## Concepts deliberately not used
- Specific poker scenarios as core illustrations (skill uses non-poker examples for accessibility)
- "Just bet" language as casualness
- Treating EVERY decision probabilistically (overkill for values / aesthetics / preference)
- Alliance for Decision Education branded curriculum
