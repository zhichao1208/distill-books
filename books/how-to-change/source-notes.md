## Critique sources used (honest-scope)

- DellaVigna & Linos (2022). RCTs to Scale: Comprehensive Evidence from Two Nudge Units. *Econometrica.* Finding: published nudge effects ~6× larger than all-trials average; ~70% attributable to publication bias × low power.
- Maier et al. (2022). No evidence for nudging after adjusting for publication bias. *PNAS.*
- Anett John (2018). When Commitment Fails. Yale working paper. Failure modes of commitment devices for present-biased agents.
- Beeminder critical review (Bethany Soule, 2021) — single mention of replication crisis in the book; flags Crum & Langer 2007, Crum 2011 as weak / unreplicated.

## Concepts NOT used and why

- **Specific company / org case studies** (Walmart vaccination, 24 Hour Fitness deployment specifics) — these are operational details for behavioral-science teams, not actionable for individual readers.
- **The author's biographical anecdotes** (Princeton OR, McKinsey, Wharton tenure path) — not load-bearing for the skill.
- **Detailed StickK / Beeminder product comparisons** — the skill warns against forfeit contracts for the vulnerable; doesn't recommend a specific product.
- **The forward by Duckworth** — Grit is a separate skill in this slate; the cross-reference is light.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Milkman is the **researcher among popularizers**. Atomic Habits (Clear), Tiny Habits (Fogg), and Power of Habit (Duhigg) are written by a journalist, a behavior designer, and a journalist respectively. Milkman is a Wharton professor running pre-registered megastudies. Her tools are situational levers — different lens than Clear's identity or Fogg's celebration. The skill is the obstacle-diagnostic complement to those identity / celebration / loop frameworks.
