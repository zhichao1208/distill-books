# Die With Zero · sources and chapter mapping

## Book

- **Original**: *Die With Zero: Getting All You Can from Your Money and Your Life*
- **Author**: Bill Perkins
- **Publisher**: Houghton Mifflin Harcourt, 2020
- **ISBN**: 978-0358099765
- **Chinese edition**: Multiple translations in market; verify before citing
- **Author background**: Hedge fund manager (Skylar Capital), energy trader, professional poker player; high net worth himself (which matters for skill framing)
- **Goodreads**: ~4.20 / 5
- **Reception**: Cult bestseller in personal finance / FIRE adjacent communities; Tim Ferriss and Joe Rogan amplifiers

## CRITICAL: who should NOT read this book

The skill's most unusual design feature: explicit exit-routing for users who would be harmed by the book. Documented here for transparency:

| User category | Why book is harmful | Skill action |
|---|---|---|
| Under-savers (no emergency fund, no retirement runway, high debt) | "Spend more" advice on top of under-saving = old-age destitution risk | Hard route to Psychology of Money skill |
| Meaning-from-work / faith / craft (not experiences) | Book's hedonist-utilitarian frame doesn't fit | Respectful exit |
| Frail-tail expectation (dementia history, disability, longevity outliers) | Time-bucket model assumes predictable health; doesn't handle late-life cognitive decline or extended care | Honest about book's blind spot |
| Acute financial crisis | Wrong tool entirely | Route to crisis-routing in PoM skill |
| Cultural / generational context where giving while alive is taboo or destabilizing | The give-while-alive recommendation requires specific family / cultural conditions | Acknowledge cultural variance |

This is the most explicit "wrong reader" routing in the repo. Mindset's honest-scope warning was about academic over-claim; this is about real material harm to vulnerable readers. The book is GENUINELY harmful for under-savers, and the skill must say so.

## The counter-frame to Psychology of Money (declared openly)

This is the most important skill-level design decision: Die With Zero and Psychology of Money are explicitly opposite frames. The skill declares this openly because users who read one and then the other often experience confusion.

| Dimension | Psychology of Money (Housel) | Die With Zero (Perkins) |
|---|---|---|
| Core question | "How do I build wealth and not lose it?" | "How do I deliberately spend wealth down?" |
| Core lens | Wealth is what you don't see; enough is a state | Time is scarcer than money; experiences > unspent wealth |
| Best for | Accumulation phase; consumption-anxious users | Decumulation phase; over-savers |
| Risk if misapplied | Over-cautious user gets stuck saving forever | Under-saver gets harmful "spend more" advice |
| Hard rule | Never specific investment advice | Honesty about who shouldn't read the book |
| Typical reader | 25-50 building wealth; questioning consumption | 45-70 already wealthy; questioning frugality |

A user with both books in their hands should know:
- PoM if you're still building "enough"
- DWZ if you've passed "enough" and aren't living
- BOTH if you're middle-aged with a stable position questioning both directions

The skill routes users between the two skills when state-first reveals which they actually need.

## Chinese edition notes

The book has Chinese editions; the title is variously translated, sometimes 《一生的财富规划：让你的钱花得有意义》 or similar — the Chinese title softens "Die With Zero" which is too blunt for Chinese self-help market norms. The blunt English title is part of the book's identity; Chinese readers should know.

Cultural note: the give-while-alive recommendation has different reception in cultures with strong intergenerational financial expectation patterns (e.g., adult children supporting elderly parents being normative). The skill is implicitly Western / Northern European in its framing of inheritance and family money dynamics.

## Important limitations / counterpoints

- **The book is written from a hedge-fund-manager / poker-player perspective.** High-risk-tolerance, high-net-worth, individualist. Most readers are not this person. Skill flags.

- **The "die with zero" target is mathematically impossible to hit precisely.** Perkins knows this; the slogan is directional. Some readers take it too literally and under-save tail-end. Skill cautions.

- **The frail-tail / dementia / late-life-care blind spot is real.** Average late-life care costs in many developed countries are $50k-$200k/year, and they're unpredictable. Perkins waves at this but doesn't resolve it. Users with family history of dementia or chronic illness should NOT take the spend-down literally without specific planning. Skill flags in not_for.

- **The give-while-alive math depends heavily on tax / legal / family conditions** that vary enormously. Skill never gives specific amounts; just frames the question.

- **The frame is hedonist-utilitarian.** Some readers find this unfulfilling because their meaning comes from work, faith, craft, or service rather than from experiences. Skill flags this mismatch.

## Related / complementary books

- **Your Money or Your Life** (Robin & Dominguez, 1992) — FIRE precursor; "life energy" framing parallel to Perkins
- **Stop Acting Rich** (Stanley, 2009) — different angle on over-spending / fake wealth signaling
- **The Millionaire Next Door** (Stanley & Danko, 1996) — empirical work on under-spenders (which DWZ would call "over-savers")
- **The 4-Hour Workweek** (Ferriss, 2007) — adjacent on time vs money trade-offs
- **Atomic Habits** (Clear, 2018) — for users who decide to spend deliberately and need to build the new habit
