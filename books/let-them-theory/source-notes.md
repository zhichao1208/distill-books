## Critical disclosure (load-bearing for skill design)

### Origin and credit controversy

**The "Let Them" concept did NOT originate with Mel Robbins.**

- **Cassie B. Phillips** wrote a viral "Let Them" poem in 2022, well before Robbins's book. The poem went widely viral on social media and is the proximate source of the popular "let them" phrasing.
- Robbins's 2024 book did not originally credit Phillips. After public controversy, Robbins acknowledged Phillips's contribution.
- This was covered in mainstream press (Yahoo Entertainment, Best Life, The Haven, and others).

**The deeper conceptual ancestor is Stoicism**:
- Epictetus's *Discourses* / *Enchiridion* (~125 CE) frames the dichotomy of control: "Some things are up to us, some things are not." This is functionally equivalent to the "let them / let me" structure.
- Marcus Aurelius's *Meditations* applies similar principles.
- Modern Stoic-inspired writers (Ryan Holiday, Massimo Pigliucci, William Irvine) have brought this to contemporary audiences. The book does not strongly credit this lineage either.

**The skill cites both** in the boot message because honest skill design requires it. Calling "Let Them" "Mel Robbins's theory" without attribution is editorially false.

### Methodology depth criticism

Multiple reviewers have noted the book extends a 2-word slogan to 300+ pages:

- Some reviewers (Psychology Today's Sage Justice column, kerserker on Substack) called it essentially "30-40 pages of useful content padded to book length"
- The actionable methodology is one cognitive interrupt phrase; the rest is anecdote, example, and elaboration
- This is a pattern in Robbins's bibliography (The 5 Second Rule, 2017, also extended one concept to book length)
- "The Let Them Theory Is Just Stoicism With Better Marketing" (kerserker, Substack) makes the case directly

The skill is honest about this in two ways:
1. The boot explicitly says the methodology is thin
2. The skill itself is **deliberately narrower** than other skills in the slate — 2 scenes instead of 3, simpler cold-start, less complex state-first table. The skill doesn't pad to match other slate entries.

## Chinese edition notes

As of mid-2026, the Chinese edition may or may not be released. Translation considerations:

- "Let them" → "让他们去吧" / "随他们" / "由他去" — all somewhat lose the imperative crispness; Chinese readers may experience this differently
- "Let me" → "让我" — works
- The Stoic ancestry is more recognizable in Chinese intellectual context (similarities to Daoist 无为 / wuwei, though not identical)

## Related / complementary books

- **Cassie B. Phillips's viral "Let Them" poem (2022)** — the proximate source; widely available
- **Epictetus, *Discourses* / *Enchiridion*** — the deep ancestor
- **Ryan Holiday, *The Obstacle Is the Way* / *Daily Stoic*** — modern Stoic-inspired
- **Massimo Pigliucci, *How to Be a Stoic*** — accessible academic Stoic introduction
- **William Irvine, *A Guide to the Good Life*** — modern Stoic synthesis
- **The Courage to Be Disliked** (Kishimi & Koga, 2013) — Adlerian, similar permission-to-disengage frame
- **The Subtle Art of Not Giving a F*ck** (Manson, 2016) — adjacent territory; thicker methodology
- **Boundaries** (Townsend & Cloud, 1992) — different but related; the boundaries literature has 30+ years of clinical backing Robbins is implicitly competing with
- **The 5 Second Rule** (Mel Robbins, 2017) — same author, same pattern (concept-to-book extension)

## Important limitations / counterpoints (load-bearing)

- **The phrase is NOT Robbins's invention.** Cassie Phillips + Stoic ancestry. Skill credits in boot.

- **The methodology IS thin.** One cognitive interrupt phrase + extended commentary. The skill matches the depth honestly (narrower than other skills in slate).

- **The slogan is DANGEROUS if misapplied** to abuse, harassment, responsibility, crisis situations. Skill explicitly refuses these cases (scene `when-let-them-is-wrong.md`).

- **Robbins's brand-marketing posture** ("life-changing tool millions can't stop talking about") oversells. The skill is calibrated to actual utility (modest cognitive interrupt for specific kinds of rumination), not to the marketing.

- **Workplace application requires nuance** beyond what the book provides. The skill flags managerial responsibility cases.

- **Some clinical practitioners worry** that simple slogans crowd out the deeper therapeutic work needed for chronic rumination, especially when ADHD / depression / anxiety / trauma is the underlying driver. The skill routes to clinical when those signals appear.
