## Concepts NOT used and why

- **Mouth-taping as default recommendation** — flagged by AASM, AADSM; carry as test-case for honest scope, route to sleep study.
- **"Ancestral palate decline" framing** — speculative chapter; refuse forwarding.
- **Mewing / orthotropics** — Mike Mew lost license; not science; refuse.
- **Right-nostril digestion / left-nostril creativity claims** — pseudoscience; refuse.
- **Holotropic breathwork as a casual recommendation** — too risky to forward without clinical container.
- **The Stanford "experiment" as evidence** — N=2 anecdote, not RCT; mention only to explain context.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Breath sits in the health/body archetype as #3, alongside Outlive (the Medicine 3.0 / Centenarian Decathlon frame) and Why We Sleep (sleep architecture and tactics). Breath is the practice-by-practice "evidence ladder" book — its lasting value is the slow-exhale / nasal-default core, surrounded by chapters that range from defensible to pseudoscience. The skill exists because the book's reach (~2-3M copies, the TikTok mouth-tape wave) created widespread misapplication that the evidence-graded skill format directly corrects.
