## Critical honesty
- **"Passion is wrong" framing oversells.** Newport's actual claim is "passion is OUTPUT not INPUT" — more defensible than pop summary "follow your passion is bad advice."
- **Framework fits skill-based / craft fields best** (STEM, writing, consulting, academia, design). Under-fits service workers, caregivers, contingent / gig creative careers where capital doesn't compound the same way. Skill flags.
- **Newport's examples are STEM/academic heavy** — advice for very different career shapes is thinner.
- **The Mission framework (Rule 4)** is less developed than Rules 1-3; some critics find it the weakest part.

## Concepts deliberately not used
- Specific career-capital-tracking templates (too prescriptive)
- "Passion mindset is morally wrong" framing (book's actual stance is more nuanced)
- Newport's specific deliberate practice prescriptions (those belong to Ericsson + Deep Work skill)
