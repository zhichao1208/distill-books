## Concepts NOT used and why

- **"80% hidden job market" claim** — folklore-grade (traced to 1970s outplacement industry); the underlying point (referrals matter) is true; the precise number is not. Skill flags this in Boot.
- **Mind maps as primary tool** — practitioner heuristic; lower-evidence; the skill mentions in Core but doesn't lead with.
- **Workshop / coaching certification funnel** — disclosed in Boot as context, not used as skill mechanism.
- **Building a team chapter** — light use; the skill is built for individual use, not for cohort design.

## Why this book and not adjacent ones

Designing Your Life is the first book in the **life-design archetype** (new in batch 3). It complements the career archetype (Range, So Good, Drive) by addressing the prior question: not "what skills to build" or "what match to find," but "what shape of life am I prototyping toward?" The skill exists with explicit honest-scope acknowledgment because the methodology was built and tested on Stanford undergraduates — broader generalization is asserted, not demonstrated.
