## Chinese edition note

The Chinese edition translates "identity-based habits" as "基于身份的习惯." The "vote for who you want to become" line is the book's most powerful, and the Chinese version is faithful to it.

The Chinese title "原子习惯" (atomic habits) is more direct than the English (原子 = literally "indivisible particle"). The skill uses "atomic = smallest unit" as its second cold-start lens.

## Related / complementary books

- **Tiny Habits** (BJ Fogg, 2019) — More radical version of the 2-minute cut; academic basis for "do it small"
- **The Power of Habit** (Charles Duhigg, 2012) — Cue / Routine / Reward three-stage; Atomic Habits adds Craving / Identity on top
- **Hooked** (Nir Eyal, 2014) — Product-side habit loop; useful for understanding why bad habits stick

Future v1.0 could add cross-references to a Tiny Habits skill.

## Important limitations / counterpoints

- **OCD / perfectionism users**: "Never miss twice" can mutate into a forced rule and reverse into pressure. Documented in EVAL.md, flagged in not_for.
- **Clinical depression**: identity-vote framing fails or harms in a "I don't want to become any version of myself" state. Flagged in not_for.
- **Structural poverty / instability**: environment design + habit stacking assume controllable environment. Doesn't apply to homelessness / refugee / extreme instability. v0.1 skill doesn't address.
