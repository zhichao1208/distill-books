# Nonviolent Communication · sources

## Book
- *Nonviolent Communication: A Language of Life*, Marshall B. Rosenberg, PuddleDancer Press, 2003 (3rd ed 2015)
- ISBN: 978-1892005281 · Chinese: 《非暴力沟通》, 华夏出版社, 2009
- Author: Rosenberg (1934-2015), clinical psychologist + international mediator; CNVC (Center for Nonviolent Communication)
- Goodreads ~4.36 / ~70k ratings
- Translated into 35+ languages; used in conflict-resolution worldwide

## Core methodology
- **OFNR (Observation / Feeling / Need / Request)** — load-bearing framework, single sentence template
- Observation vs Evaluation (separating fact from judgment)
- Feelings vocabulary (book includes specific lists)
- Universal Human Needs (book lists ~9 categories: connection, physical, autonomy, meaning, play, honesty, peace, etc.)
- Requests vs Demands (request = OK to say no; demand = consequence if refused)
- Giraffe vs Jackal metaphor (Rosenberg's playful framing; in-group jargon)
- Empathy (the specific NVC version: hearing other's OFNR)
- Self-empathy (turn OFNR inward)
- Anger as gateway to unmet need
- Use cases: conflict, mediation, intimate relationships, parenting, organizational

## Critical honesty (LOAD-BEARING)
- **Cult-like reception in some communities** — method gets applied dogmatically. "You're not being NVC" becomes its own judgment. Rosenberg himself cautioned against this.
- **POWER-IMBALANCE FAILURE MODE** — NVC assumes good faith. In abuse / coercive control, method can be USED AGAINST the less-powerful party ("you're being violent in your communication"). Skill REFUSES OFNR application to abuse.
- **Robotic OFNR-script in intimate relationships** can paradoxically distance partners — formal speech feels artificial. Skill teaches underlying move without requiring formal script.
- **Universal Needs list is empirically thin** — useful heuristic, not validated taxonomy.
- **"Violence" framing can be moralistic** — make ordinary disagreement feel pathological.

## Concepts deliberately not used
- Giraffe/jackal metaphor (in-group jargon; off-putting to non-NVC readers)
- "You're being violent" judgments toward user's non-NVC communication
- Cult-style dogma about NVC purity
- Specific CNVC certification track

## Pairs with
- **Crucial Conversations**: complementary; CC stronger for workplace, NVC stronger (with caveats) for intimate / family
- **Difficult Conversations** (Harvard Negotiation Project): more academic alternative
- **Boundaries** (Townsend & Cloud): different framing; some friction with NVC about how to handle non-good-faith counterparts
